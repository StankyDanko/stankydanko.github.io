A — Evolved Cyberpunk

Keep the current synthwave/neon DNA but grow it up. Less "stoner blog," more "underground tech artist who happens to be a sentient plant." Dark backgrounds, neon accents, but clean typography and professional project showcases. The personality lives in the copy and micro-interactions, not in visual noise.

Pros

  • Must be careful not to go too "gamer" for investors

B — Clean & Botanical

Strip away the cyberpunk. Go minimal, light, organic. Think botanical illustration meets modern portfolio. The "plant" aspect becomes a subtle design motif (leaf patterns, growth metaphors, green palette) rather than the main personality. Professional first, personality in the details.

Pros

  • Most broadly professional
  • Easy to read, fast to load
  • Won't scare off any audience

Cons

  • Loses what makes StankyDanko StankyDanko
  • Looks like every other portfolio
  • Feels like conforming — the thing you don't want

C — Dark Studio

GitHub-dark aesthetic meets creative studio. The vibe of a late-night workshop where serious tools get built AND albums get produced. OMNI-blue (#58a6ff) as the primary accent. Clean, structured, but with edge. The personality comes through in what you've built, not decorative effects. Projects speak louder than animations.

Pros

  • Projects are the hero — your work sells itself
  • Dark but not niche — developers and creatives both feel at home
  • Scales well as you add more projects
  • The OMNI connection makes your ecosystem feel intentional

Cons

  • Less visually wild than current site
  • Plant persona is subtle, not front-and-center

My recommendation: C (Dark Studio) — it lets your work do the talking while keeping the edge. But A could be incredible if we execute it well. B is safe but it's not you.
